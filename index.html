CD Rates Calculator



The Certificate of Deposit (CD) calculator can show you how much interest you can earn with this savings product.

How to use:

  1. Step 1: Enter your initial deposit amount under the Initial Deposit box.
  2. Step 2: Add the number of years for the CD to mature and the compound per year.
  3. Step 3: Add the interest rate.
  4. The results will display the total after the numbers of years inputted and the APY Rate.

    5. Close

HELOC Calculator



A home equity line of credit, or HELOC, is a special type of home equity loan. Rather than borrowing a specific sum of money and repaying it, a HELOC allows you to borrow money as needed, up to a certain limit, and repay it over time. It's like having a credit card secured by your home equity.


How to use:

  1. Step 1: Enter interest rate, length of your loans in years, add any other closing costs costs, and your tax rate.
  2. Steps 2,3,4: Enter any loan accounts you already have such as auto loans, boat, credit cards .. etc. You can choose to keep boxes blank if not applicable.
  3. The results will show a comparison between Debt and HELOC. The comparison will include monthly payments, average interest rate, payoff timeline, total monthly payments and the average annual tax savings. The results will also display the total loan amount.

Close

Rent vs Buy Calculator



Should you buy or rent? This is a question most of us will likely face in our lives, whether buying a house makes more financial sense than renting a home. There is a way to understand the financial impact of buying vs. renting. The rent vs. buy calculator can help you calculate the net cost of buying a home versus the cost of renting over time.


How to use:

  1. Step 1: Enter your Monthly Rent and your Annual Rent Increase expected.
  2. Step 2: Enter the Home Value and Annual Maintenance, set the Annual Appreciation percentage for the home then choose the number of years before sell and set the Selling Cost percentage
  3. Step 3: Under loan information, select Loan Amount, interest rate length and the points.
  4. Step 4: For taxes and insurance information, set the Tax Rates percentage and Annual Taxes percentage,and type the value for the Annual insurance.
  5. Results will show you the total payment for both renting and buying, average monthly payment, monthly rent savings and tax saving, total rent savings, house appreciation value, proceeds minus costs, loan balance equity appreciation and home purchase benefits.

    6. Close

Credit Card Payoff Calculator



The Credit Card Payoff Calculator will show you how long it will take to pay off your credit card debt.


How to use:

  1. Choose from making the minimum payment, a fixed amount of your choosing or a time when you would prefer to be debt free.
  2. Step 1: Enter your current credit card debt balance, and your current monthly payments toward it.
  3. Step 2: Enter your annual interest rate and your payoff goal for how many months you want to pay off your debt in.
  4. Results will show you the payment required to reach your payoff goal, payoff timeline, total interest saved, total payments, the time saved compared to your previous payments, and interest savings.

    5. Close

How Much House can You Afford Calculator



This calculator can assist you in determining the price range of the house that you can afford with your current financial status.


How to use:

  1. Step 1: Enter the home value for the property you are interested in with the desired down payment amount.
  2. Step 2: Enter additional loan information. The length of the loan in years, the interest rate , the estimated front ration and the estimated back ratio in the boxes, respectively.
  3. Step 3: Enter taxes and insurance information. The annual taxes, annual insurance and the annual PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance).
  4. The results will show the monthly principal and interests, monthly real estate taxes, monthly insurance, loan to value ratio, months with PMI, total monthly payments, allowable debt payments and finally the minimal required income to be able to afford the mentioned expenses.

    5. Close

What if I Pay More Calculator?



What if I Pay More Calculator enables you to test additional payments that you will be making on top of the existing regular monthly payments and the overall impact it has on the loan.


How to use:

  1. Step 1: Enter your loan amount.
  2. Step 2: Enter the interest rate of that loan.
  3. Step 3: Enter the length of the loan in years.
  4. Step 4: Enter any additional payments.
  5. The results will show a comparison between the standard payment and the additional payment, the comparison will include monthly payments, total monthly payments and length. The results will also display the interest savings and the time saved.
